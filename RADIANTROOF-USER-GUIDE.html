Roles & where the app sends you

After sign-in

RadiantRoof uses your account role to choose a sensible home experience:

  • Administrator — directed to the Admin console to manage users and inventory.
  • Investor — directed to the Investor portal for portfolio-oriented views.
  • Standard user (“flipper”) — directed to the Workflow dashboard for sourcing through renovation.

Admins can still open the workflow dashboard and investor areas from their navigation when those links are shown.

🔐

Signing out

From the global header, Logout ends your session and returns you toward the public home experience.

Home (landing)

/

The home page introduces Radiant Roof Realty with a rotating inspirational quote, animated background visuals, and short “blessing” cards. It is primarily marketing and orientation, not operational deal work.

What you do here: read the message, then choose sign in or register to enter the product. A simple Todos widget may appear for lightweight task tracking on the marketing surface.

Outcome: you reach authentication or account creation and move into role-appropriate tools.

About & Contact

/about · /contact

About summarizes the product’s purpose: supporting high-ROI real estate evaluation, workflow, and investor visibility.

Contact provides a short form (name, email, message). Submitting shows a thank-you confirmation on the page. In a full deployment this would typically connect to email or a ticketing backend.

Register & Login

/register · /login

Register collects your name, email, password, confirmation, and intended role, then creates an account through the server. Successful registration shows a confirmation and routes you to login.

Login verifies email and password. While signing in you see a short loading state; on success the app routes you by role (see Roles & where the app sends you).

Outcome: an authenticated session with access to dashboards, properties, and tools permitted for your role.

Global header & Quick Deal Calculator

All authenticated pages

The top header is always available (wrapped by the root layout). It includes quick links to:

  • Properties — catalog of listings from the server.
  • Offline Data — local property records when you are disconnected or want a scratch list.
  • Investors — investor-facing portal entry.
  • Contact and Settings — communication and personal / data tools.
  • Quick Deal Calculator — opens an overlay for fast numeric checks without leaving your current page.

When signed in, contextual buttons appear: Workflow (flipper home), Admin (admins), Investor (investor role), and Logout.

🧮

Quick Deal Calculator

Use this when you need a rapid sanity check on numbers while browsing elsewhere. It is a companion tool, not a replacement for the full Analysis workspace.

Properties (listings catalog)

/properties

This page loads properties from the API and presents them in a rich table view with portfolio-style statistics: counts by status, totals, averages, and price bands.

Filters let you narrow the grid to For Sale (Available), Pending, and/or Sold—toggle combinations to focus the list.

Outcome: a clear inventory picture for underwriting support, marketing review, or admin oversight—grounded in server data rather than your private deal scratchpad.

Offline Data

/offline

When connectivity is limited or you want a private local list, use Offline Properties. You can add simple records (address, price, description), save them to local storage, and remove entries you no longer need.

Outcome: a lightweight CRM-style scratchpad that does not depend on the live server—ideal for field notes that you may later formalize into real listings or deals.

Settings (app & data)

/settings

The general Settings area focuses on your working environment and data custody:

  • Preferences such as theme, notifications, language, and optional auto-backup flags (stored locally where configured).
  • Encrypted backup export — package workspace-related data into a protected file you can archive.
  • Restore / import — bring data back from a backup with password verification and merge vs replace choices (as presented on screen).

Outcome: control over how the app feels day-to-day and a path to disaster recovery or device migration without losing your local workflow artifacts.

Deal memory (current deal)

Across dashboard

Many workflow screens share a single “current deal” concept: as you promote a lead, the app keeps key numbers together (address, asking price, ARV, repair estimates, phase).

That context persists in the browser so Acquisition and Renovation can pick up where Screening left off. A history list retains recent deals for recall.

Outcome: less re-typing between stages; always verify numbers before offers or draws, since persistence is device-local until deeper server integration is wired everywhere.

Workflow dashboard shell

/dashboard/*

Authenticated flipper users see a dedicated layout: left sidebar (branding, profile chip, stage links, tools, logout), top summary strip (headline stats and quick actions like Add Property / Reports / Alerts), and the main canvas for the active step.

Badges beside stages are illustrative counts in the current UI—use them as visual priority hints rather than guaranteed live totals unless your deployment connects them to analytics.

Dashboard overview

/dashboard

The overview welcomes you by name and surfaces portfolio KPI cards (properties, active deals, profit potential, ROI). A horizontal pipeline strip previews each stage with sample cards and deep-links into the corresponding workflow page.

Additional panels highlight recent activity, quick actions (add property, import, schedule), and calendar-style awareness.

Outcome: at-a-glance orientation before you dive into a specific stage.

Pipeline view

/dashboard/pipeline

The Pipeline is a Kanban-style board: columns for Source → Screen → Analyze → Acquire → Renovate → Exit. Each property card shows price, ARV, profit, ROI, and a priority treatment (for example “Hot”).

You can drag cards between columns or use stage action buttons on cards (e.g., move to Screen, Make Offer, Update Progress) depending on the column.

Outcome: a single visual control tower for where every opportunity sits in the lifecycle.

Sourcing

/dashboard/sourcing

Sourcing is where opportunities enter the funnel. The page is organized by tabs such as lead tiers (warm vs distressed vs cold outreach), import, wholesaler network, expired listings, and county records—mirroring common acquisition channels.

Each lead card shows score, financial hints (asking, ARV, quick repair estimate), and actions. Start Screening creates or updates the current deal in context so downstream pages inherit the property story.

Outcome: qualified leads become structured deals ready for diligence in Screening.

📥

Imports & feeds

Use import-style tools to bulk-ingest leads when you have spreadsheets or lists; confirm field mapping on screen before trusting large batches.

Screening

/dashboard/screening

Screening is your property-level diligence desk. Pick the active property, refine ARV and asking price, and build a line-item repair model across major systems and rooms, including a contingency percentage.

A checklist walks through data collection, comp support, market health, and the 70% rule gate. Toggling checklist items helps your team see what is complete.

Outcome: a documented go/no-go package with a defendable repair budget before full underwriting in Analysis.

Analysis

/dashboard/analysis

Analysis deepens the numbers using tabs:

  • Deal calculator — purchase, ARV, repair stack (with contingency), holding and closing costs, financing charges, equity and ROI storylines.
  • Repairs — detailed breakdown consistent with screening.
  • Comps — comparative sale context for ARV support.
  • Financing — structure sensitivities for debt and carry costs.
  • Scoring — weighted qualitative factors (location, condition, market, numbers, exit) combined into a deal score.

Outcome: a unified underwriting narrative you can align with capital partners before making an offer in Acquisition.

Acquisition

/dashboard/acquisition

Acquisition translates underwriting into actionable offer strategy. The page hydrates from your current deal when available (address, ARV, repairs with contingency, holding/closing, profit targets).

You can compare offer scenarios, inspect maximum allowable offer logic relative to goals, choose a financing product (rates, points, LTV, payment), and review timelines and next steps such as earnest money and inspection milestones.

Outcome: a documented offer posture linked to financing and profit constraints—ready for negotiation with the seller side.

Renovation

/dashboard/renovation

Renovation is the execution command center. Tasks inherit from the deal’s repair structure and move through states like pending → bidding → bid awarded → in progress → completed.

For each line you can track estimates, lowest bid, bid counts, and duration; actions include starting bidding workflows, kicking off work, and marking completion with optional progress bars.

The Budget Tracker contrasts total budget vs spend; the Phase Timeline shows macro phases (demo, rough-in, finishes, punch) with percent complete.

Outcome: controlled spend, visible contractor competition placeholders, and a clear path from rehab start to listing readiness (handoff toward Exit).

Exit stage

Linked as /dashboard/exit

The workflow sidebar and several cards reference an Exit step for disposition (listing, buyer offers, closing the flip). In the shipped routes, treat Exit as the conceptual final column after renovation.

⚠️

Implementation note

A dedicated Exit page may not be present in all builds; if the link errors, track disposition tasks within your team’s external MLS/title tools until an Exit screen is added.

Investor portal

/investors/*

Investor accounts (and admins reviewing as investors) use a green-themed portal shell with its own sidebar. Implemented child pages include:

  • Portfolio — toggles between a Deal Analyzer view and a portfolio list using shared investor components.
  • Deal Flow — dedicated DealFlowDashboard experience.
  • Transactions — transaction history table for capital movements.

The sidebar also advertises Funded Properties, Returns & Projections, and Communications. Those destinations are navigation targets—confirm in your build that each route is deployed before training users on them.

Outcome: investor-grade transparency into positions and cash events, parallel to the operator workflow.

Admin console

/admin/*

Administrators get a separate layout with glassmorphism styling and quick actions. Core areas:

  • Admin dashboard — KPIs (users, properties, revenue-style metrics, approvals), recent activity feed, shortcuts to add inventory or export.
  • Properties — server-backed grid with stats, edit/delete pathways, and links to Add Property flows.
  • Users — list, create, and remove accounts with modal-driven forms and validation.
  • Admin settings — system-oriented panels (stats placeholders, backup/export concepts, operational toggles as shown).

Outcome: centralized control of who can access the system and which inventory records exist—plus visibility into aggregate health.

FAQ & best practices

Use Properties for authoritative inventory synced from the server. Use Offline Data for quick captures in the field or when you intentionally want records that stay on this device.
Acquisition may apply contingency-loaded repair totals or refreshed defaults. Always reconcile against your latest underwriting workbook before releasing an offer.
User Settings backups target client-side packages (preferences and packaged data as implemented). Server-grade backups are an operations concern—coordinate with whoever hosts your RadiantRoof server database.

Interactive: switch lenses on the workflow—operator vs capital partner.

Day-to-day focus: Sourcing → Screening → Analysis → Acquisition → Renovation. Keep the current deal updated so offer math and rehab tasks stay aligned.
Investor focus: Portfolio composition, deal-flow dashboards, and transaction history—verify reporting cadence with your sponsor operator.
